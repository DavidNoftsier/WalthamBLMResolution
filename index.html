Black Lives Matter Resolution

Waltham, MA - June 2020

BLACK LIVES MATTER

The Resolution

On June 8th, 2020, during the bi-weekly Waltham City Council Meeting, Councillor Kristine Mackin of Ward 7 proposed a resolution made up of two parts:
  1. The City Council joins the Police Department in denouncing police violence and brutality against Black people, and affirms that Black Lives Matter, and reaffirms that Waltham is a Welcoming Community

  2. The City Council will work with the Police Department alongside community leaders and advocates to understand the steps the Police Department has taken and can take to provide effective public safety, while ensuring accountability not only to the City Council and Mayor's Office, but to the public as a whole and the community of Waltham

The Vote

President Paul Brasco and Vice President Kathleen McMenimen then discussed the logistics of bypassing the normal committee process by voting to act on the resolution immediately. Councillor Mackin expressly stated that she desired for council to vote and act on the resolution in order to immediately agree to the first part:
  1. The City Council joins the Police Department in denouncing police violence and brutality against Black people, and affirms that Black Lives Matter, and reaffirms that Waltham is a Welcoming Community
and that it would be acceptable for the second part to go through the regular committee process where it could be discussed in more detail before bringing it before the Committee as a Whole.

The Problem

In the past two years, 40 resolutions have been voted on during Waltham City Council Meetings without the resolution going through the regular committee process. This type of process change is more typical when the issue at hand is pressing and should be expedited. Not a single one of the 40 resolutions that have been voted on in this manner were voted down.

However, when the City Council voted on whether to act immediately on the Black Lives Matter resolution, all councillors voted 'no' except for Councillor Mackin and co-sponsor Councillor Jonathan Paz of Ward 9.

Why did the majority of the councillors vote no to acting immediately and affirming that Black Lives Matter?

What part of Black Lives Matter is up for discussion?

Since the council voted 'no', the resolution has been moved to a committee panel for discussion before it will again be brought before the entire City Council. The Public Works & Public Safety Committee is meeting June 15, 6:00PM at 119 School St, Waltham for their first discussion on the BLM resolution.

The public is welcome to attend.
